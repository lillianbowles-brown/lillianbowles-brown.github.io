About Me

I’m a first-generation, non-traditional Univeristy of Utah graduate who spent 15+ years in financial services and 10 years running a successful e-commerce business before returning to school. I earned my B.S. in Political Science and Sociology, Cum Laude, driven by curiosity, a love of learning, and a desire to understand people, systems, and social impact.

I previously served as an AmeriCorps VISTA with the Utah Refugee Services Office and Women of the World, where I supported program evaluation, community building, leadership development, and volunteer coordination. This experience strengthened my interest in data, strategy, and the role that research plays in creating meaningful impact.

I am currently a full-time caregiver for my mother while continuing to build my technical and leadership skills. Even in this season of caregiving, I remain committed to lifelong learning—exploring tech, data, AI tools, and policy to better understand how technology can support community impact and expand future career possibilities.
